Staple Crops of India

  • Rice

    A staple food for over 60% of Indians, grown mostly in West Bengal, UP, Punjab. It needs high rainfall and warm temperatures.

  • Wheat

    A key winter crop, mainly cultivated mostly in Punjab, Haryana, MP, UP. It requires cool temperatures and fertile soil.

  • Maize

    It is used for food, fodder, and industry, grown mostly in Karnataka, AP, Bihar. They are adaptable to kharif and rabi seasons.

  • Barley

    It is used in brewing and livestock feed, mostly cultivated in Rajasthan, UP, MP. They thriving in cool, dry climates.

  • Jowar

    Satiate your palate and take you on a culinary treat.

  • Chickpea

    A protein-rich pulse, mostly grown in Madhya Pradesh, Rajasthan, Maharashtra. They need dry climates.

  • Pigeon Pea

    A major dal variety, mainly produced in Maharashtra, MP, Karnataka. They require moderate rainfall

  • Black Gram

    It is essential for dal, mostly grown in MP, UP, Tamil Nadu. It prefers dry conditions and loamy soil.

  • Lentil

    A winter crop, produced in UP, MP, Bihar. It needs cool weather and moderate rainfall.

  • Turmeric

    Packed with anti-inflammatory properties, vitamins, and minerals. Mostly grown in Kerala, Karnataka, AP. They need warm, humid conditions.

  • Green Gram

    High in protein, mostly cultivated in Rajasthan, Maharashtra, Andhra Pradesh. They thrive in warm temperatures.

  • Mustard

    Used for oil production, cultivated in Rajasthan, Haryana, MP. They grow well in dry, cool climates.

  • Groundnut

    A major oilseed crop, mostly grown in Gujarat, Andhra Pradesh, Tamil Nadu. They need sandy soil and moderate rainfall.

  • Soybean

    Used for oil production, cultivated in Rajasthan, Haryana, MP. They grow well in dry, cool climates.

  • Sunflower

    Grown for edible oil in Karnataka, Maharashtra, AP. It requires moderate rainfall and sunlight.

  • Sesame (Til)

    Used for oil and sweets, cultivated in UP, MP, Rajasthan. They need warm weather and sandy soil.

  • Sugarcane

    India’s major cash crop, mostly grown in UP, Maharashtra, Karnataka. It requires high water availability.

  • Cotton

    Used for oil and sweets, cultivated in UP, MP, Rajasthan. They need warm weather and sandy soil.

  • Jute

    Essential for textiles, mostly cultivated in Gujarat, Maharashtra, Telangana. They thrive in black soil and warm climates.

  • Tea

    A key export, mostly produced in Assam, West Bengal, Kerala. They require high humidity and rainfall.

  • Coffee

    A commercial crop mainly grown in Karnataka, Kerala, Tamil Nadu. They thrive in cool, shaded hills.

  • Banana

    India’s top fruit crop, grown in Tamil Nadu, Maharashtra, Kerala. It requires warm climates.

  • Mango

    The king of fruits, mostly cultivated in UP, Maharashtra, Andhra Pradesh. They thrive in tropical conditions.

  • Coconut

    A plantation crop, mainly produced in Kerala, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka. It requires a warm climate and well-drained soil.
